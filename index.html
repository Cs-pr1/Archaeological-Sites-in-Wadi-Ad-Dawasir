Wadi Ad Dawasir

Location

Wadi Ad Dawasir is bordered to the north by Al Quwaiiyah Province, to the south by the Najran Region, to the east by Al-Sulayyil Province and Al-Aflaj Province, and to the west by the Asir Region and the Makkah Region. The area of Wadi Ad Dawasir is approximately 48,900 square kilometers. In the past, the valley was traversed by flowing streams, sloping from the west to the east, including the flow of Wadi Tathlith, Wadi Ranya, and Wadi Bisha.

Climate

Wadi Ad Dawasir experiences a desert climate, characterized by high temperatures and low precipitation. Temperatures range from 5 degrees Celsius in winter to 45 degrees Celsius in the summer season.

Al Faw Village

Al Faw Village in Wadi ad Dawasir is a picturesque settlement nestled within the captivating landscapes of Saudi Arabia. Surrounded by the rugged beauty of Wadi ad Dawasir, this village exudes charm with its traditional architecture and cultural richness. The village is a testament to the region's historical and heritage significance, offering a glimpse into the local way of life. Visitors can explore narrow alleys, adorned with ancient structures, and engage with the warm hospitality of the locals. Al Faw Village stands as a tranquil oasis in the heart of the desert, providing an authentic experience of Saudi Arabian culture and history.

Al Jaw Archaeological Village

The archaeological village of Jaw is located northwest of Wadi ad Dawasir, and it is currently known as Al-Mulqatah. The area is recognized as one of the richest archaeological sites in Wadi ad Dawasir, containing ancient artifacts. It is certain that this site includes traces of civilizations predating Islam and may even date back to pre-Christian eras.

Al-Rabie Palace

The history of Al-Rabie Palace dates back to the time of the First Saudi State and is attributed to the renowned preacher Sheikh Rabie bin Zaid Al-Makhraji. He played a significant role in promoting the teachings of Sheikh Muhammad bin Abdulwahab during his era. The palace consists of private residences and guest quarters, featuring four towers in its corners. Each tower comprises two floors, with an additional lower basement used as a warehouse for weapons and supplies. The second floor of the towers served for surveillance and guarding purposes. In the southern part of the palace, there is a stable for horses.

Al Sadria Museum and Resort

The Al Sadria Museum and Resort showcases models representing civilizations from various countries, including a design of the Eiffel Tower, sculptures depicting customs and cultures of numerous nations such as the Chinese dragon, a gate representing Palmyra in Syria, a council chamber reflecting the Malaysian system, as well as an Asirian and Najdi house. The resort includes furnished rooms and a lounge for visitors. Inside, there is a small museum housing over 2000 archaeological pieces and 5000 coins from different eras.
